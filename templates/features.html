ü§ñ AI Sentiment Analysis

How It Works

Every journal entry is automatically analyzed using VADER (Valence Aware Dictionary and sEntiment Reasoner), an AI model specifically designed for understanding emotions in everyday language.

Real-time Analysis: The moment you save an entry, our AI reads your words and determines the emotional tone.

What We Measure

üòä Positive Sentiment

Words expressing happiness, gratitude, excitement, love, and optimism

Example: "amazing," "grateful," "wonderful"

üòî Negative Sentiment

Words expressing sadness, frustration, anger, worry, and disappointment

Example: "frustrated," "disappointed," "worried"

üòê Neutral Sentiment

Factual, informational, or balanced emotional tone

Example: "went to work," "ate dinner"

The Sentiment Score

Each entry receives a compound sentiment score from -1.0 to +1.0:

+0.05 to +1.0
Positive - Happy, grateful, excited entries
-0.05 to +0.05
Neutral - Balanced or informational entries
-1.0 to -0.05
Negative - Sad, frustrated, worried entries

What You Get

  • Daily sentiment tracking - See your emotional journey over time
  • Monthly trends - Identify patterns in your mood
  • Yearly summaries - Understand your emotional year at a glance
  • Visualizations - Charts and graphs showing sentiment trends

üí¨ POV (Point of View) Sharing

Share specific thoughts with friends without sharing your entire entry.

How to Share a POV

Use the special {pov} syntax in your journal entries:

Today was an amazing day! I went to the beach.

{pov} @alice @bob
Just wanted to share - saw the most beautiful sunset today!
Reminded me of when we all went camping last year.
{/pov}

The rest of my entry stays private...
What happens: Only Alice and Bob will see the POV content. It gets automatically added to their journal entry for that same date, clearly marked with your username.

Key Features

  • Selective Sharing - Share paragraphs, not entire entries
  • Friends Only - Can only share with confirmed friends
  • Auto-Injection - Content appears in recipient's journal for that date
  • Clear Attribution - Recipients see who shared it
  • Reply Threads - Recipients can reply, creating conversations
  • Notifications - Friends are notified when you share

Recipient Controls

‚úÖ What Recipients Can Do
  • Delete POVs from their journal anytime
  • Reply to shared POVs
  • Choose to ignore POVs
  • Report inappropriate content
‚ö†Ô∏è What You Can't Do
  • You cannot unsend a POV once shared
  • You cannot force delete from recipient's journal
  • You cannot see if they've deleted it
‚ö†Ô∏è Think Before You Share: POV content becomes part of your friend's journal. While they can delete it, you cannot control what they do with it once shared. Only share what you're comfortable with them keeping.

üîí Privacy & Encryption

Your thoughts are safe. Here's exactly how we protect your most private writing.

üîê Encryption at Rest

All journal entry titles and content are encrypted before being stored in our database using Fernet encryption (AES-128 in CBC mode).

  • Database administrators cannot read your entries - even with database access
  • Backups are encrypted - your data is safe even in backups
  • Decryption happens only when you access your entries

ü§ñ How AI Works with Encryption

You might wonder: "If my entries are encrypted, how does AI analyze them?"

  1. When you save an entry, it's temporarily decrypted in memory
  2. The AI analyzes the text and generates sentiment scores
  3. The scores are saved (these are just numbers, not your actual text)
  4. Your entry is re-encrypted and stored
  5. The decrypted version never touches the disk

This means AI features work perfectly while maintaining encryption at rest.

üåê Encryption in Transit

  • HTTPS/TLS - All data transmitted over secure connections
  • Secure cookies - Session data protected from interception

üë• What About Shared POVs?

POV content is also encrypted at rest, just like your regular entries. When you share a POV:

  • The content is encrypted in your friend's journal
  • Only you, your friend, and the AI (for analysis) can read it
  • Database admins still cannot read shared content
